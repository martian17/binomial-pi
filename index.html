Calculating π using random walk and normal distribution

π = 1/(2*σ^2*f(μ)^2) where σ is the standard deviation, f(μ) is the probability distribution on the average point

brief description:

This program calculates π using random walk and normal distribution.
Given a fact that random walk results in a normal distribution, we can exploit this to calculate pi by reversing the equation of normal distribution which looks like the following.
f(x) = 1/(σ√(2π))*e^(-1/2*((x-μ)/σ)^2)

in which
f(x) is the probability distribution,
σ is the standard deviation,
μ is the average.

notice
f(μ) = 1/(σ√(2π))
therefore
π = 1/(2*σ^2*f(μ)^2)

we can calculate μ and σ with commonly known means, and then we can calculate f(μ) by takeing a small strip from the distribution center, and dividing it with its width.
